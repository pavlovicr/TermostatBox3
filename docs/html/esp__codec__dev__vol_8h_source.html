39/*
40 * Audio gain overview:
41 * |----------------Software Gain--------------|--Hardware Gain--|
42 *
43 * |--------------------| |--------------------| |------------------| |---------| |----------------|
44 * | Digital Audio Data |-->| Audio Process Gain |-->| Codec DAC Volume |-->| PA Gain |-->| Speaker Output |
45 * |--------------------| |--------------------| |------------------| |---------| |----------------|
46 *
47 * Final speaker loudness is affected by both Software Gain and Hardware Gain.
48 *
49 * Software Gain (Adjustable):
50 * Audio Process Gain: Gain by audio post processor, such as ALC, AGC, DRC target MAX Gain.
51 * Codec DAC Volume: The audio codec DAC volume control, such as ES8311 DAC_Volume control register.
52 *
53 * Hardware Gain (Fixed):
54 * PA Gain: The speaker power amplifier Gain, which is determined by the hardware circuit.
55 *
56 * The speaker playback route gain (Audio Process Gain + Codec DAC Volume + PA Gain) needs to ensure that the
57 * speaker PA output is not saturated and exceeds the speaker rated power. We define the maximum route gain
58 * as MAX_GAIN. To ensure the speaker PA output is not saturated, MAX_GAIN can be calculated simply by the formula.
59 * MAX_GAIN = 20 * log(Vpa/Vdac)
60 * Vpa: PA power supply
61 * Vdac: Codec DAC power supply
62 * e.g., Vpa = 5V, Vdac = 3.3V, then MAX_GAIN = 20 * log(5/3.3) = 3.6 dB.
63 * If the speaker rated power is lower than the speaker PA MAX power, MAX_GAIN should be defined according to
64 * the speaker rated power.
65 */
